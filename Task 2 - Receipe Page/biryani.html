Biryani Recipe



Certainly! Here's a simple and delicious recipe for vegetable biryani along with the ingredients you'll need:

    Ingredients:
    For Rice:
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 cloves
  • Salt to taste

      • Vegetable Mix:
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes, etc.), chopped
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

      • For Biryani Layering:
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 cup fried onions (optional)
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for garnish, optional)

      • Instructions:

    Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes. In a large pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice along with bay leaf, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, cloves, and salt. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% cooked. Drain the excess water. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and cook until the raw smell disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and the oil starts to separate. Add mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are partially cooked. Stir in the yogurt, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. In a small bowl, mix garam masala with a tablespoon of water to form a paste. Add this paste to the vegetable mix and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). In a large oven-safe dish, layer half of the partially cooked rice at the bottom, followed by the vegetable mix. Top it with the remaining rice. Drizzle ghee or oil on top and sprinkle fried onions (if using). Optionally, you can also add saffron-soaked milk for a rich flavor. Cover the dish with aluminum foil or a tight-fitting lid and bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Once done, let the biryani rest for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy your delicious vegetable biryani!
